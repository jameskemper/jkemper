About the WRECKEM Model

This project combines my passion for March Madness with data analytics and is, hopefully, a neat educational tool for my students. The WRECKEM (weighted ratio efficiencies comparison model), named in honor of Texas Tech University, is designed to forecast outcomes of NCAA Men's Basketball games.

The model incorporates concepts from Luke Benz's YUSAG model, the ELO ranking system, and NCAA Net Rankings. It assesses the impact of a team and their opponent’s offensive and defensive efficiencies, game location, strength of schedule, and historical performance on the point differential for a game.

After calculating the various statistics, the model utilizes a straightforward weighted regression analysis:

$$ PD = \beta_{\text{Team}} X_{\text{AdjOffEff}} - \beta_{\text{Opp}} X_{\text{AdjDefEff}} + \beta_{\text{Loc}} X_{\text{Loc}} + \epsilon $$

  • PD: Point differential, defined as the difference in points scored by the two teams in a game.
  • XAdjOffEff: Adjusted offensive efficiency
  • XAdjOffEff: Adjusted offensive efficiency of the team.
  • XAdjDefEff: Adjusted defensive efficiency of the team.
  • XLoc: The game location variable.
  • βTeam: Coefficient quantifying the impact of the team's adjusted offensive efficiency.
  • βOpp: Coefficient quantifying the impact of the opposing team's adjusted defensive efficiency.
  • βLoc: Coefficient quantifying the impact of the game location.
  • ε: The error term, capturing unobserved factors affecting the game's outcome.

Using the regression results, the model runs hundreds of iterations for each game to account for the unpredictability inherent in sports. These simulations provide a probabilistic forecast for future outcomes.
